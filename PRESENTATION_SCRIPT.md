# PRESENTATION SCRIPT: ScotiaSeed (10 Minutes, Strict)

**Purpose.** Word-for-word delivery script with timing, transitions, and movement cues. Designed to land in 10 minutes with breathing room. Practice three times before stage.

**Constraints from the case (page 13).** Strict 10-minute limit, you will be cut off. Have the prototype open before you walk on. Bring own laptop. Five-minute Q&A follows.

**Voice rules.** No em-dashes anywhere. Short sentences. Concrete numbers. Pause after the hook. Pause before the close.

**Cast.** Recommended structure for a four-person team:
- **Speaker A**: opens (hook through slide 3), delivers the close. The most confident speaker.
- **Speaker B**: slides 4 through 6 (the wedge and the mechanic).
- **Speaker C**: slide 7 (the flow demo) and slide 8 (the numbers).
- **Speaker D**: slide 9 (alignment and timeline) and primary Q&A backstop.

If you are a three-person team, Speaker A also takes slide 9.

## SLIDE 1. THE MAYA HOOK
*(Speaker A. 50 seconds. Open with stillness. No movement until "Wealthsimple" lands.)*

> Imagine someone.
>
> Twenty-two years old. Scotia chequing customer since she was sixteen. Banks on her phone every day.
>
> Last Tuesday, she decided to start investing. She opened Wealthsimple.
>
> *(Pause. Two full seconds.)*
>
> Not Scotia.
>
> *(Pause again.)*
>
> Wealthsimple.
>
> Today, Scotia has 1.25 million customers exactly like her. Wealthsimple is acquiring 80,000 of them every quarter.
>
> We are not here to ask how to take customers from Wealthsimple. We are here to ask how to stop giving them away.
>
> *(Transition.)* I want to show you what is actually happening.

**Movement.** Stand still. The hook does not need gestures. The pause is the work.

**Why this opens differently from the other 39 teams.** Other teams will open with a statistic, a quote from the case, or a generic "young Canadians want to invest" framing. Maya is a person. She becomes the lens for every number you cite in the next nine slides. When you say "1.25 million" in slide 2, judges will see Maya multiplied.

## SLIDE 2. THE GAP
*(Speaker A. 55 seconds.)*

> Wealthsimple was founded twelve years ago. Today its assets under administration are one hundred and twenty-five billion dollars, up seventy-one percent in the last year alone.
>
> One in five Canadians between eighteen and forty already uses Wealthsimple. That is not market share. That is a generation choosing.
>
> And one trillion dollars is moving from baby boomers to younger Canadians by the end of this year, according to CPA Canada. That is twenty Wealthsimples worth of capital changing hands.
>
> The institutions that build investment relationships with younger Canadians now will manage that trillion dollars for the next forty years.
>
> Scotia has the deposit. Wealthsimple has the investor.
>
> *(Transition.)* So why is Scotia losing this conversation?

**Pointer.** Briefly walk to the screen and gesture at the $125B figure when you say it, then return to center.

## SLIDE 3. WHY THEY ACTUALLY DISENGAGE
*(Speaker A. 60 seconds.)*

> The case answers this directly, and the answer is not what you might think.
>
> Forty-five percent of Canadians say they are not confident in their investment knowledge.
>
> Forty-three percent of younger Canadians say they do not know where to begin.
>
> Eighty-four percent of eighteen to thirty-four year olds report negative emotions about RRSP contributions. The highest of any age group.
>
> And only thirty-six percent of that same age group is confident about basic retirement mechanics. Compare that to sixty-nine percent of Canadians over fifty-five.
>
> *(Look up. Direct eye contact with judges.)*
>
> From the case, page six: "The barrier to first investment among younger Canadians is more often confidence and clarity than it is capital."
>
> So we built a program that solves for confidence and clarity, using the rails Scotia already has.

**Transition.** "Here is the unfair advantage Scotia already has and does not use." Speaker B steps forward.

## SLIDE 4. OUR INVISIBLE WEDGE
*(Speaker B. 70 seconds.)*

> Five things fintechs structurally cannot match.
>
> *(Count them off on your hand if comfortable.)*
>
> One. Pre-verified identity. If you already bank with Scotia, we already verified your ID under FINTRAC Method 5. Wealthsimple has to do that from scratch. We collapse onboarding from twenty-five minutes to five.
>
> Two. One point one million dollars of stacked protection. CDIC on cash, CIPF on securities, native to Scotia. Wealthsimple engineered this through partner banks; Scotia gets it built-in.
>
> Three. The FHSA. The First Home Savings Account is the killer registered product for our demographic, because the bulk of FHSA contributors are aged twenty-five to thirty-four. It solves the housing-anxiety and retirement-anxiety double-bind in one product.
>
> Four. Instant internal transfers. Wealthsimple Roundup pulls from external accounts in a weekly batch, one to three day delay, NSF risk. Scotia moves money internally, in real time.
>
> Five. Trust where trust is lowest. Eighty-two percent of eighteen to twenty-four year olds get their financial information from social media. Scotia is the credible alternative they already chose for banking.
>
> *(Transition.)* Here is how we use those five advantages.

**Pointer.** As you say each number, walk a step closer to the screen. By "five" you should be at the screen, pointing at the trust card.

## SLIDE 5. THE MECHANIC
*(Speaker B. 70 seconds.)*

> ScotiaSeed is a two-year matched-contribution program.
>
> When a Scotia customer between eighteen and twenty-four opens their first investment account, we give them twenty-five dollars instantly.
>
> When they complete five short education lessons, five minutes total, plain language, we give them another twenty-five.
>
> When they set up a recurring monthly contribution of at least twenty-five dollars and we see three months of consistent deposits, we give them fifty dollars.
>
> At the twelve-month anniversary, if they are still contributing, another hundred.
>
> At the twenty-four month graduation, if they are still contributing, a final hundred.
>
> Three hundred dollars total, paid only on engagement, plus two cents per Scotia purchase routed into their investments, capped at fifty dollars across two years.
>
> *(Slow down. This is the strategic insight.)*
>
> The bonus is the hook. The recurring contribution is the moat. Vanguard's data on automatic enrollment shows nine in ten people stay enrolled three years later. Defaults beat incentives.
>
> *(Transition.)* But the most important part of this program is what the customer sees.

---

## SLIDE 6. THE TWO-LEDGER UI (HERO)
*(Speaker B. 75 seconds.)*

> *(Gesture to the phone mockup on screen.)*
>
> This is what a ScotiaSeed user sees when they open the app.
>
> On the left, in charcoal: your money. Your deposits, your round-ups, your growth. Liquid. You can withdraw any of it, any time, no questions, no penalties.
>
> On the right, in Scotia red: Scotia's match. Visible from day one. Locked for twenty-four months.
>
> *(Pause.)*
>
> This is the trust mechanism. Most financial apps hide what you do not get. We show it. The locked match is the carrot you can see growing every month.
>
> The forfeiture rule is simple and disclosed in plain language: if you withdraw an amount that touches Scotia's portion, you have a ninety-day window to restore it. After that, the remaining match is reclaimed and the program ends. No clawback. No surprises. Just a clear line.
>
> Behavioral finance calls this "loss aversion with dignity." You are not punished for needing your own money. You are reminded what you are walking away from.
>
> *(Transition.)* Now I want to show you how fast someone gets to this dashboard.

**Hand-off.** Speaker C steps forward.

## SLIDE 7. THE FLOW
*(Speaker C. 60 seconds.)*

> Five screens. Under five minutes. From Scotia chequing customer to invested.
>
> Screen one. The hook in the Scotia app. The customer sees that they qualify for ScotiaSeed because they already bank with us.
>
> Screen two. We surface the identity we already have on file. Name, date of birth, SIN, address, employment. They confirm with one tap.
>
> Screen three. A five-question CIRO suitability assessment, in plain language. "If your investment dropped twenty percent next month, what would you do?" with four real-feeling options, including "honestly, I do not know" because that is a normal answer.
>
> Screen four. The algorithm recommends the account that fits. For an eighteen to twenty-four year old saving for a house, that is almost always an FHSA. We make FHSA the hero.
>
> Screen five. Twenty-five dollars unlocked. The first contribution lands in Scotia Essentials Portfolios automatically. We prompt them to set up the monthly contribution that earns the next bonus.
>
> *(Transition.)* The mechanic works. The flow is fast. So why should Scotia spend the money?

## SLIDE 8. THE NUMBERS
*(Speaker C. 80 seconds.)*

> Three hundred dollars per fully-converted user, paid only on engagement, plus fifty dollars in round-up funding. Three hundred and fifty dollar ceiling.
>
> Here is what Scotia is already spending and what competitors spend.
>
> Scotia's current student promo: one hundred and seventy-five dollars per youth chequing account opened, with two activation actions, through July of this year. Already on the books.
>
> Wealthsimple's referral entry bonus: twenty-five dollars for a hundred dollar deposit.
>
> RBC Direct Investing's current offer: up to five thousand dollars cashback on a ten thousand dollar locked deposit.
>
> ScotiaSeed at three hundred is forty percent below Scotia's existing student spend and ninety-four percent below RBC's investing CAC ceiling.
>
> Now what does Scotia get back.
>
> Banking-industry customer lifetime value: between two thousand and forty-five hundred dollars per retail relationship. Add the FHSA-to-mortgage attach, fifteen hundred dollars on average. Add wealth-transfer optionality, embedded. Conservative twenty-year NPV at a seven percent discount rate: three to six thousand dollars per converted user.
>
> Three hundred in. Three thousand to six thousand out.
>
> *(Pause.)*
>
> A ten-to-twenty-times return on a customer Scotia already has.
>
> *(Transition.)* And it builds on Scotia's own strategy.

**Hand-off.** Speaker D steps forward.

*(Speaker D. 60 seconds.)*

>
> "Earn primary client relationships." ScotiaSeed converts a deposit relationship into a primary investing relationship, before fintechs do.
>
> "Make it easy to do business with us." ScotiaSeed collapses investment account onboarding into a two-minute upgrade of an existing chequing relationship.
>
> And we can build this in eighteen months because nothing here is new product development. Every component already exists at Scotia.
>
> Q3 of this year: build the matching engine, the two-ledger UI, the FINTRAC reuse API, the education modules.
>
> Q1 next year: pilot with twenty thousand students in Scotia's existing Preferred Package for Students and Youth. Validate the retention curve.
>
> Q3 next year: broaden to eighteen to twenty-four non-students.
>
> Q1 of 2028: full national launch.
>
> Pilot exposure: seven million dollars, ceiling, on Scotia's seven point eight billion in net income. Less than one tenth of one percent.

> *(Transition.)* I want to close with the reason this matters.

## DURING-PITCH HAZARDS

If the timer hits eight minutes and you are only at slide 7, cut slide 8 to thirty seconds: "$300 in, $3K to $6K out, ten to twenty times return." Then slide 9 in thirty seconds: "Maps to two of Scotia's pillars, eighteen months to launch." Then the close.

If a slide fails to advance, do not freeze. Say "while we get that back" and deliver the slide content from memory. Most judges will respect this.

If you forget a stat, do not guess. Say "the precise number is in our deck" and continue. Then have a teammate surface it in Q&A.

---

## Q&A POSTURE

The first question is the hardest. Take a half-second beat before answering. The 5-minute clock is forgiving; deliberate beats rushed.

Use the headline answers in `03_ScotiaSeed_QA_Defense_Pack.md`. Lead with one sentence, stop, let the judge follow up.

If a judge says "I disagree with X," do not capitulate and do not stonewall. Say "you may be right; here is the evidence we used to land where we did," then cite. This shows you are not brittle.

End strong. If you have a final beat in Q&A, redeliver the elevator pitch:

> Scotia already has 1.25 million young Canadians as deposit customers. Wealthsimple is acquiring 80,000 of them as investment customers every quarter. ScotiaSeed converts our existing customers into investors using rails Scotia already owns, in under five minutes, for less than Scotia already spends to acquire a single student chequing account.

That is the line they remember when they score.
