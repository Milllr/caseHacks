## 1. THE CASE IN ONE PARAGRAPH

Scotiabank has 18 to 34 year old Canadians on its books as deposit customers, but is losing them to Wealthsimple and Questrade for investment products. Wealthsimple has grown to 3 million Canadians and over $100 billion in assets under management, almost entirely on the demographic Scotia is failing to convert. The intergenerational wealth transfer of over $1 trillion is happening from 2023 to 2026 (CPA Canada). Whoever owns the investment relationship now will manage that capital for decades. Scotia's solution must be compatible with existing infrastructure, viable in 18 months, and move the needle on sustained engagement (not just account openings).

---

## 2. OUR ANSWER IN ONE PARAGRAPH

**ScotiaSeed** is a 2-year matched-contribution program for Scotia's existing 18 to 24 year old deposit customers. It converts banking customers into investors using rails Scotia already owns. The user opens a Scotia investment account (default: Scotia Essentials Portfolios inside a FHSA) in under five minutes, with their identity pre-filled from existing banking via FINTRAC Method 5. They get $25 instantly. They earn up to $275 more across the next 24 months in milestone unlocks (education complete, recurring contributions set up, 12-month anniversary, 24-month graduation), capped at $300 lifetime. On every Scotia debit purchase, Scotia routes 2 to 3 cents into their investment account (capped at $50 over 2 years). The dashboard shows two side-by-side ledgers: "Your money" (deposits, round-ups, growth, fully liquid) and "Scotia's match" (locked for 24 months). If a user withdraws into Scotia's portion, they have a 90-day window to restore it or they forfeit the program. The bonus is the hook. The auto-PAC is the moat. The two-ledger UI is the trust.

## 7. THE FOUR INVISIBLE WEDGES (USE WHEN ATTACKED)

When asked "what stops Wealthsimple from copying this," answer with these four structural moats in this order.

1. **Pre-verified identity (FINTRAC Method 5).** Wealthsimple must do fresh identity verification on every new customer. We re-use ours. Cuts onboarding from 25 minutes to 5.
2. **Instant internal transfers.** Wealthsimple Roundup pulls from external accounts on a weekly batch (1 to 3 day delay, NSF risk). Our round-ups move internally in real time.
3. **CDIC plus CIPF stacked protection.** $100K on cash plus $1M on securities, all native at Scotia. Wealthsimple had to engineer this through partner banks.
4. **Trust at a moment of low trust.** 82 percent of 18 to 24 year olds get financial information from social media. Scotia is the credible counter-position they already chose for banking. Wealthsimple is 12 years old; Scotia is 193.

Even if Wealthsimple copies the $25 plus matching mechanic perfectly, they pay it to *acquire* a new customer; we pay it to *deepen* an existing one. Different unit economics.

## 8. THE THREE LIVE LANDMINES IN SCOTIA'S CURRENT PRODUCT

If a judge tries to trap you with "but doesn't Scotia already do X," here is what is actually true.

1. **Scotia Smart Investor's default recommender may route a youth saver to a GIC.** $500 minimum, CDIC-insured, wrong vehicle for long-horizon wealth building. ScotiaSeed overrides this by defaulting first-time investors with horizons over 3 years to Scotia Essentials Portfolios.
2. **iTRADE has a quarterly maintenance fee on registered accounts under a balance threshold.** Wrong for a beginner with $50 in their account. ScotiaSeed avoids this by routing through Smart Investor first.
3. **CIRO suitability cannot be skipped.** Even with FINTRAC reuse, every new investment account requires a fresh suitability questionnaire. We compress it (5 minutes, plain language) but do not eliminate it.

## 9. VOICE EXAMPLES (USE THESE PATTERNS)

When writing new slide copy or speaker notes, mirror these patterns.

Headline pattern.
- "Scotia has the deposit. Wealthsimple has the investor."
- "It is not the money. It is the confidence."
- "Five things fintechs cannot match."
- "Two ledgers. Total trust."

Body pattern. Short sentences. Concrete numbers. No corporate adjectives.
- Good: "Scotia is 193 years old. Wealthsimple is 12."
- Bad: "Scotia brings best-in-class institutional trust to the next-generation investor."

Pull-quote pattern. Always credit the source.
- "Quote from case." then on the next line: "Scotiabank case package, page 6."

CTA pattern.
- "Open in 60 seconds."
- "Set a monthly contribution."
- "Start your future."

Avoid every time.
- "Leverage," "synergy," "disrupt," "best-in-class," "revolutionary," "game-changing," "innovative," "cutting-edge," "industry-leading," "transformative," "robust."
